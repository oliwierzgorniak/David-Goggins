Introduction

My favorite athlete is an American ultra-runner, ex-Navy-Seal, an authentic influencer who lives his painful maxims every day - David Goggins. He is a brave advocate of challenging yourself to become a better, more disciplined person. His struggle in life led him to conclusions which for many are hard to practice – to become better you need to challenge your mind and go further than your body tells you. One of his discoveries is that when you feel like you are done with something you have only reached 40% of your potential. With his warrior mindset David Goggins accomplished outstanding challenges like: Badwater 135 (135 miles run in Death Valley), Hell Week, world record in pull ups in 24 hours.

Childhood

Abusive father

David did not have an easy start to life. The atmosphere in his home was tough. David’s father – Trunnis was a corrupt person who forced his children to work in the nights during school days and beat them up. The violence was also addressed to Jackie Goggins – David's mother. The 8-year-boy lived in constant fear. The beat ups had a serious impact on Goggins mental health, because of them Goggins suffered trauma even in his adult life. One day his mother found enough courage to run away from his abusive partner.

After the escape

Jackie Goggins and David moved to Brazil, Indiana. Finally, Goggins got rid of their huge problem – Trunnis, but life did not turn into a wonderland. They still had serious challenges. One of them was racism. Brazil was a small town with almost no black people. Americans' mentality back then was much different than today. Racism was a common phenomenon. The pinnacle of it was a situation in which a teenager pointed a gun at Goggins' head while he was walking down the road with his black friend.

Educational struggles

The other David’s struggle was his situation at school. He was a slow learner. It took him much more to acquire material than other kids. He needed attention from others and should have put in a lot of work to keep up. Otherwise, he would have ended up in a special school which he described like that: “It was like a bad movie. The shrink set up seven chairs in a semicircle around him, but some of the kids wouldn’t or couldn’t sit still. One child wore a helmet and banged his head against the wall repeatedly. Another kid stood up while the doctor was mid-sentence, walked toward a far corner of the room, and peed in the trash can. The kid sitting next to me was the most normal person in the group, and he had set his own house on fire!”. To avoid such a place he chose cheating, which he practiced for a long time. This decision had adverse consequences on the man’s life.

Transformation and military career

Time for change

As a young men David decided to join the Air Force inspired by his proud Grandfather who served there for 37 years being a cook. But there was an obstacle in his way. He failed to pass the ASVAB test, which is an equivalent of SATs (exam used for college admission in the US) for the military. David studying struggles and constant cheating led to the situation where he could not get 36 points out of 99 need to join the Air Force. The change was coming. Young David was in a desperate situation. He had bad grades at school, missed a lot of classes and had no future. That day Goggins, in front of a mirror, gave himself a self-motivation talk and decided to be disciplined and pick up life’s challenges. He became a different person. He trained hard in the gym, ran around, and studied for the ASVAB. His third attempt to pass it was a successful one. This allowed him to enter the army.

Career

David started his military training, where he was exceptionally good in sit-ups, push-ups, running, but swimming was his Achilles’ heel. He felt relieved as the doctors discovered Sickle Cell Anemia, because of this Goggins stopped the training. After a while, the medical team reversed the decision. David could join the program again, but he would have had to start all over again. Future warrior still had a quitting mentality and decided not to go back. After that he moved to Fort Campbell, Kentucky and served working with air jets. He was upset with his decision and ran away into weight training and eating. That is how he grew to 300 pounds (136 kg). After 4 years in the army Goggins became a rat exterminator – a job which is not respected in society.

Joining Navy Seals

He lived that kind of life, when one day he saw a TV program with Navy Seals. At that moment David felt bad about his job and environment. He wanted a change and saw a chance to achieve this by joining the special operations unit. There was a problem. Big problem. David had to lose 106 pounds (48 kg) to join the Navy Seals program. First, he had to start with cycling. He could not run because of his weight. Overall, he did at least swimming, weightlifting, biking, and after losing weight running. He got this, but still there was an educational barrier. He had to pass the ASVAB test again. For Navy Seals he needed a better score than before. After solid studying he did it as well. This crazy transformation was done. He could enter one of the best if not the best special operations units in the world.

Being badass

Navy Seals training

This training is not a joke. The 6-month program is divided into 3 phases: physical training, dive training and land warfare. The first phase only ~20 % of people pass. Maybe because this phase consists of the famous Hell Week, which is a 130-hour constant nightmare. During Hell Week potential future Seals are exposed to chilly water to the extent that there are doctors who do hypothermia checks. They also run on sand with boats or logs and do other draining physical exercises. It turned up that Goggins participated in 3 Hell Weeks, which two of them he completed.

San Diego One

One day David decided to look for the toughest races and he found Badwater 135. In this event runners must beat 135 miles in one of the harshest places on earth – Death Valley. Goggins contacted the organizer but to join he needed to have run experience. Future ultra marathon runner did not give up. He decided to participate in an ultra-running event – San Diego One Day. In this event runners have 24 hours to run/walk for as long distance as they can. Goggins started having a fast tempo but after a while problems occurred. David ran 70 miles (~112 km) without preparation and the previous day he had a serious weight training with his friend from the army. His body was in such a bad condition that he involuntarily defecated on the chair that he was sitting on. Despite that he chose to go back to the race. In pain he managed to get missing 30 miles.

Ultra races and philosophy

After this event David discovered a new potential in him. He knew that he could do far more than he thought he can. This is one of his rules - if you think you are done then you achieved only 40 % of your potential at that moment. After this race Goggins did not stop running. He competed in such races as: HURT 100, MOAB 240 and the previously mentioned BADWATER 135. He does such tough things in order to “callous his mind.” David gives this name for the process where you challenge yourself, feel pain to later be more resilient to it. He did not stop at just running. He participated in triathlons and broke the record of pull ups done in 24 hours. He is a versatile athlete who achieves those victories because of his warrior mindset.

Conclusion

I admire David Goggins’ attitude toward struggle. He is a hero, who shows that radical change in life is possible. He is an example to many young men for whom it seems not ok to follow today’s voices praising mediocrity. I try to choose a harder path because of him like many other individuals. Although Goggins achieved those things, which I wrote about, a long time ago he is still active, and you can get motivated by seeing his struggle on Instagram. Although I do not think he is a perfect man, for sure one of the bad traits is swearing, you can gain a lot of good things from reading to his story and implementing some of his strategies written in one of his books - “Can’t Hurt Me. Master Your Mind and Defy the Odds.”, which I really recommend.
